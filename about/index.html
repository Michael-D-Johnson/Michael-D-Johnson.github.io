The Data

Data for this visualization was taken from the American Housing Survey: https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/hads/hads.html. This dataset includes a wide range of national housing affordability metrics starting from 1985 to 2013 covering odd years. The dataset includes information about cost of housing, burden of housing (housing cost/income), owning versus renting, and subsidized housing.
In the top chart, “Average Household Salary per Income Bracket: Owners vs. Renters, 2001-2013,” it appears that owners and renters have been affected similarly by the recession (the same trends are seen in owners and renters for each income bracket). It is clear from the plot that owners consistently have higher income than renters. This is expected because owning a home is a big investment that usually demands greater income. Most notably the upper three income brackets show a sharp recovery post-recession compared with the lower brackets, which show more gradual (if any) recovery.
